We start with the 47 central midfielders who commanded the ball most during the tournament.

Act II: The Final Third Threat

Progressing the ball in your own half is easy. Progressing it where it hurts is elite.

We zoom in on our Top 9. To reach the Final 4, players must avoid the Red Elimination Zone by heavily penetrating the Final Third or directly into the Penalty Box.

Notice how Højbjerg and Casemiro fall into the red, while Pedri, Modrić, Kimmich, and Eriksen survive.

Act III: The Tactical DNA

We have our Final 4. But how do they actually play?

The Pass Sonar (Left) shows their passing habits. The slice length shows the percentage of passes in that direction, and color shows distance.

The Pitch Map (Right) shows exactly where their vertical passes land on the grass (gold = into the box).

Pedri controls the edge of the box, Eriksen strikes straight up the middle, Modrić offers total balance, and Kimmich rains fire directly into the penalty area.
